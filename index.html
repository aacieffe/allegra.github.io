The Problem

STARTING FROM ZERO

The client operated in the automotive sector without adequate digital tools to manage either company fleets or the relationship with end users. No centralised platform existed — fleet operators worked with fragmented processes, while drivers had no digital access to information about their own vehicle. Everything had to be designed from scratch, for two very different user types: B2B fleet operators and B2C end users.

🖼 Add your discovery / problem framing image here
The Process

HOW I WORKED

01
DISCOVERY

Stakeholder interviews and analysis of existing operational workflows. Requirements gathering for both user types. Competitive analysis to identify established patterns and areas for improvement.

02
DEFINITION

Separate user flow mapping for operators and end users, with attention to touchpoints between the two systems. Functional prioritisation in close collaboration with the development team.

03
DESIGN

Low-fidelity wireframes to validate architecture and flows, followed by high-fidelity mockups. Shared Design System across both products to ensure visual consistency and reduce dev time.

04
ITERATION

Structured client presentations with feedback collection. Constant communication with the development team in English. Technical documentation updated at every sprint.

The Solution

TWO PRODUCTS, ONE ECOSYSTEM

Two distinct digital products, designed consistently within the same ecosystem — sharing a Design System and a coherent visual language.

🖥
Web Application · B2B
FLEET OPERATORS PORTAL

Centralised fleet management: vehicle monitoring, maintenance planning, intervention management and operational reporting. Designed for efficiency and clarity at scale.

📱
Mobile App · B2C
END USER APP

Personal vehicle management: vehicle status, notifications, service history and access to connected services. Designed to be simple, intuitive, and genuinely useful.

The Problem

THREE USERS, ONE PLATFORM

The client needed a complete digital solution to modernise the management of smart electronic waste units across the urban collection network. No previous product existed — everything had to be designed from scratch, for three very different user types: back-office administrators, field operators, and end users (citizens). The challenge was creating three coherent products that shared a single design language.

🖼 Add your discovery / user mapping image here
The Process

HOW I WORKED

01
DISCOVERY

Stakeholder interviews to gather requirements, map existing operational workflows, and understand the needs of each of the three user groups. Competitive analysis to identify smart city best practices.

02
DEFINITION

User flow mapping for each of the three distinct touchpoints. Functional prioritisation in close collaboration with the development team to align design decisions with technical constraints.

03
DESIGN

Low-fidelity wireframes to validate structure with the client, followed by high-fidelity mockups. Creation of a shared Design System with the dev team, with a strong focus on accessibility across all three products.

04
ITERATION

Cyclic revisions based on client feedback during structured presentation sessions. Continuous update of development support documentation and interface specifications.

The Solution

THREE PRODUCTS, ONE DESIGN SYSTEM

An integrated platform made up of three distinct yet visually and functionally consistent products, all built on a single Design System to ensure coherence and scalability.

🖥
Web App · Administrators
MANAGEMENT DASHBOARD

Real-time monitoring of smart electronic units across the territory. Status visualisation, intervention management and reporting tools.

📱
Mobile App · Field Staff
OPERATORS APP

On-the-ground operational management. Simplified interface optimised for use in mobility and non-ideal conditions. Robust and fast.

📱
Mobile App · Citizens
END USER APP

Guided waste disposal for citizens. Intuitive onboarding, clear flows and accessible communication for a broad and diverse user base.
